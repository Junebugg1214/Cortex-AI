# X Article Draft: Why I Built Cortex

Most AI products treat your memory like platform exhaust.
You generate the data, but the platform controls the memory layer.

I built **Cortex** because I think that model is backwards.

## The core belief

Your AI ID should belong to you.

By AI ID, I mean the memory/context that actually makes an assistant useful:

- your preferences
- your projects and goals
- your professional history
- your technical stack
- your recurring instructions and corrections

That context is your leverage.
If it is trapped in one product, you are trapped too.

## The problem today

Moving between assistants usually means:

- copy/paste prompts
- rough import/export hacks
- lost context
- re-teaching the same things over and over

You lose time and continuity.

## What Cortex does

Cortex gives you a portable memory/identity layer you can carry across tools.

You can:

1. Connect your AI tools
2. Pull and organize memory/context
3. Curate and review your AI ID
4. Share only what is needed for each use case

Instead of dumping everything, you can share scoped slices like:

- professional
- technical
- minimal
- full

## Why self-host first

If the value is user-owned memory, the infrastructure has to match the philosophy.

That is why Cortex is now **self-host only** for storage ownership.

- your server
- your data
- your control

Not “trust us.”
Actual control.

## The connector model

Connectors run on a scheduled loop (24h by default).

- If a provider path can be automated, it can auto-sync.
- If not, there is a fallback action-required flow.

This lets users start now, and progressively move toward more automated sync paths.

## Who this is for

- People who use multiple LLM tools and want continuity
- Builders who want an open CLI-first identity layer
- Teams that need policy-scoped memory sharing
- Anyone who believes memory portability should be normal

## What this is not

- A locked assistant
- A central memory silo owned by the app creator
- Another black-box sync product

## Why this matters long-term

AI UX is becoming memory UX.

The winner is not just the best model.
It is the system that keeps your context portable, auditable, and under your control.

That is the direction.
